The Rose Garden In The City of Roses

Nestled in the scenic hills of Washington Park, the International Rose Test Garden stands as one of Portland, Oregon’s most treasured landmarks. Known proudly as the “City of Roses,” Portland celebrates its floral heritage through this remarkable garden, which draws visitors from around the world with its beauty, fragrance, and history.

Established in 1917, the garden was originally created as a safe haven for hybrid rose varieties during World War I. Today, it has blossomed into a vibrant living museum, home to more than 10,000 rose bushes representing nearly 650 distinct varieties. The garden serves as both a testing ground for new roses and a showcase of award-winning blooms, allowing visitors to experience the diversity and innovation of rose cultivation.

The garden’s thoughtfully designed terraces offer breathtaking views of downtown Portland, Mount Hood, and the surrounding landscape. From early spring through late autumn, visitors can wander along winding pathways lined with colorful blossoms, each labeled to highlight its unique characteristics. Whether it is the delicate petals of a tea rose or the rich, velvety tones of a hybrid variety, every corner of the garden offers a new visual delight.

Beyond its horticultural significance, the International Rose Test Garden is a place of relaxation and inspiration. It invites guests to slow down, breathe in the sweet aroma of roses, and connect with nature in an urban setting. Seasonal events, educational programs, and guided tours further enrich the visitor experience.

As a symbol of Portland’s identity, the Rose Garden embodies the city’s commitment to natural beauty and community pride. A visit to this floral sanctuary is not just a stroll among flowers—it is an immersion into the spirit of the City of Roses itself.
